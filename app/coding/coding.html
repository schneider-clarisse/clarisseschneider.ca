To create an open-source conference app and save nice people thousands of $$$

For our fourth year design project, my teammates Lara, Kevin, Steven, Eric, Kyle, and I created an open sourced conference web app. Calligre is a conference management suite, acting as a free and fully functional alternative for running conferences and was designed to help small, new, or student run conferences with little to no budget.

EngPort

A Matter of Great EngPort

March 28, 2014

To allow users to download a only events they're interested in

Once upon a time, I convinced my housemates Kevin and Dan to attend hackWATERLOO. As Speaker of Waterloo's Engineering Society, I needed a way to download and share events and information concerning my class, without having to deal with other events getting in the way. We sat down at 7pm, spent about 5 hours getting Rails running successfully on all of our machines, and hacked away until 10am the next morning with the help of excessive pop and energy drinks. We created a system that categorized announcements by meeting, also allowing to search by tags, and a customized pdf export design. I owe many beers to Kevin James and Dan Reynolds for their endless patience, inspiration, and apparent insanity for deciding to live with me.

OneBible

Falling In Love With FrontEnd

Nov-Dec, 2013

To offer a native Bible reader for Blackberry

The first project I contributed to was Joshua Netterfield's OneBible application, the only Bible reader for PlayBook and BlackBerry 10 with a five star rating. It was banned in several countries, much to our pride, before it had to be removed from AppWorld for legal reasons. When Josh asked me to make his app "pretty", I didn't know what I was getting into. Little by little, I helped to design a clean, easy-to-use, customizable interface in QML and Qt/C++, and I was hooked. I knew that I'd found my niche in frontend, and there was no going back.
